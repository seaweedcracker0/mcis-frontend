PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT (PDPA) 2010

Personal data is any recorded information (including an expression of opinion) relating to a living individual from which his identity can be directly or indirectly ascertained. Common examples of customers’ personal data are their names, addresses, telephone numbers, identity card numbers, dates of birth, occupations, medical records, financial information, policy information, claims information, etc.

Under the PDPA, a data user, in processing personal data, must comply with the following principles:

(1) the General Principle;
(2) the Notice and Choice Principle;
(3) the Disclosure Principle;
(4) the Security Principle;
(5) the Retention Principle;
(6) the Data Integrity Principle; and
(7) the Access Principle.
(collectively referred as “Seven Data Protection Principles”)

Failure to abide by the above principles amounts to an offence. Upon conviction, the data user is liable to a fine not exceeding RM 300,000.00 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding two (2) years or to both (Sec5(2) PDPA).

Under these principles, the collection and use of personal data must be consented to by the data subject and steps must be taken to ensure that they are stored securely. The processing of personal data cannot be excessive in relation to the purpose or related purpose of which the personal data is collected.

Adequate notice must be given to data subjects that their personal data will be processed, used, and the purpose of the same. Such notice must be in the Malay and English languages. Personal data no longer in use has to be destroyed.

As an Agent, you would in the course of your work have access to confidential information of clients especially when they fill in the proposal and fact find forms or even the claim forms or when you assist the policy holder with their medical assessment etc.

You should not disclose customers’ personal data to other companies for marketing their products, nor should you use customers’ data for purposes unrelated to the handling of the customers’ accounts with MCIS. Prescribed consent must be obtained from the relevant customer before any change of use of his data.

Documents containing personal data such as copies insurance application form or policy should be securely kept against access by unauthorized parties. Personal data stored electronically in computers or portable storage devices should be protected with adequate IT security measures and access control. File protect your communications via email if the content includes personal details of anyone.

If you are delivering any documents to or from the Policy holder please ensure that the documents are kept secure and delivered safely to the company/policy holder as soon as possible. Ensure all documents are delivered to the intended persons and no one else.

Further you need to ensure you maintain the highest professional standards at all times and do not engage in gossiping especially about your client’s confidential information nor disclose any information about a client over the telephone, social media or any other telecommunication modes unless you are sure that you know who you are disclosing the information to and they are authorised to have it.

MCIS Insurance Berhad has no tolerance for non compliance and would not hesitate to take actions against any individual who wilfully failed to comply with the requirements stated above.

As our Agent, you need to ensure that at all times you handle all personal information of policyholders as confidential, know and abide by company policy on Personal Data Protection and ensure full compliance to the Personal Data Protection Act 2010.
